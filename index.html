How to Play Freecell

Freecell is a form of solitaire played with a standard 52-card deck. The goal is simple: move all cards to the four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King.

The Basics

  • The Tableau: The game starts with all 52 cards dealt face-up into eight cascades or columns.
  • The Free Cells: There are four empty slots, called "free cells," at the top. You can temporarily store one card in each free cell. These are crucial for maneuvering your cards.
  • The Foundations: The four empty foundation piles are where you build up your suits, starting with the Ace of each suit.

The Rules

  1. You can move the top card of any cascade or any card from a free cell.
  2. Cards can be moved onto a cascade if it is one lower in rank and of the opposite color. For example, a red 9 can be placed on a black 10.
  3. A card can be moved to a foundation pile if it's an Ace (to start a pile) or one higher than the current top card of the same suit.
  4. Any card can be moved to an empty cascade.
